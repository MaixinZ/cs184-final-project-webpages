    <div className="hero-lead">
      Team Members: Xuhuan Shen, Maixin Zhang, Dantes Chen, Yimeng Li
    </div>
    <br />
    <div className="hero-lead">
      We propose a real-time graphics project that applies custom scene-level
      stylization to <em>Cyberpunk 2077</em> using a ReShade-based shader
      pipeline. Our main goal is to design several coherent full-scene visual
      styles, study how to transition between them over time, and improve
      global consistency through coarse scene-aware control rather than naive
      uniform post-processing. <em>Cyberpunk 2077</em> provides official mod
      support through REDmod, making this a realistic platform for a
      rendering-focused project. As an optional extension, we will explore
      whether stylized observations of the game can be used to fit a 3D
      Gaussian Splatting representation for novel-view rendering.
    </div>
    <div className="hero-meta">

  <section>
    <article className="paper-card">
      <span className="paper-label">Problem Description</span>
      <h2>What this project is trying to conquer</h2>
      <div className="copy-block">
        Modern commercial games already contain rich geometry, lighting, and
        materials, but most stylization work in practice is either limited to
        simple full-screen filters or requires deep access to an engine&apos;s
        internal rendering pipeline. Our project focuses on an intermediate but
        meaningful setting: scene-level stylization of an existing game in real
        time, where we do not rebuild the renderer from scratch, but instead
        design custom shader-based effects that are expressive enough to create
        distinct visual languages while still preserving scene coherence.
      </div>
    </article>
  </section>

  <section className="page-grid">
    <article className="paper-card">
      <span className="paper-label">Goals And Deliverables</span>
      <h2>What we plan to deliver</h2>
      <div className="copy-block">
        Our baseline deliverable is a working real-time stylization system in
        <em> Cyberpunk 2077 </em> using ReShade, with at least four complete
        scene-level styles. Each style should change the visual language of the
        scene in a meaningful way rather than acting like a minor color preset.
        Candidate styles include a cinematic or fantasy grading style, a
        stylized toon-like style, and a painterly or oil-paint style. We want
        to show that the same world can move across different visual states
        while remaining recognizable and coherent. We also plan to support
        scene-wide style switching and at least one form of smooth temporal
        transition.
      </div>
      <ul className="stack-list">
        <li>
          <strong>Baseline demo</strong>
          A short video or live demonstration in <em>Cyberpunk 2077</em>.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Style coverage</strong>
          At least two distinct styles applied to the same scene, with a target of four full styles in total.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Temporal behavior</strong>
          Style switching and one smooth transition over time.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Evaluation material</strong>
          Matched before and after comparisons on the same scene.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </article>

    <article className="paper-card paper-card--accent">
      <span className="paper-label">Aspirational Extension</span>
      <h3>What we hope to deliver</h3>
      <div className="copy-block">
        Our main aspirational extension is a 3DGS branch. We would capture a
        sequence of stylized observations, estimate or extract camera
        information, and fit a 3D Gaussian Splatting model using Nerfstudio&apos;s
        Splatfacto. Then we would test whether stylized observations can
        support plausible novel-view rendering. This would let us explore a
        more research-oriented question: whether a learned scene representation
        can preserve a stylized visual language rather than only a photorealistic
        one.
      </div>
      <ul className="stack-list">
        <li>
          <strong>3DGS branch</strong>
          Use Nerfstudio and Splatfacto to test stylized novel-view rendering from captured observations.
        </li>
        <li>
          <strong>Split-world scene idea</strong>
          Build our own scene in Godot and reproduce a split-scene lighting or shading effect similar to <em>Split Fiction</em>.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </article>
  </section>
